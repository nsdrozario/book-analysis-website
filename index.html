Not just any biography

Symphony for the City of the Dead reads much like a narrative novel, enticing you into the exciting story of the complex web of events during World War 2, the rise of the USSR, and the Siege of Leningrad.

About the Author

Why did M. T. Anderson write Symphony for the City of the Dead?

Anderson was a classical music reviewer and has a deep passion for classical music. Anderson, discovering the profound, unprecedented historical impact of Shostakovich's music, much like the moving nature of Shostakovich's symphonies themselves, wanted to share this unknown and surprising depth behind the events of the formation of the USSR and the outbreak of World War II.
